Should we be worried about global population growth?

From around 1500 to 1750, the global population was at an estimated 500 million. Then, from 1750 to 1950, the global population started gradually increasing and reached around 2.5 billion. From 1950 to the present day, the global population has been in exponential growth and today, the global population is estimated to be 7.9 billion. The reasons for such an increase in global population are better nutrition and advances in medicine. Therefore, fewer people are dying, and more people are being treated and saved by doctors. However, considering the global death rate is decreasing and the global birth rate has roughly stayed the same throughout history, the global birth and death rates are unequal, therefore the global population is rapidly increasing.

On one hand, I believe that we should be worried about global population growth. This is because, if the global population’s exponential growth continues, there may not be enough space or resources to accommodate everyone on Earth. It is estimated that the global population will reach 10 billion people in 2050. However, the Earth’s carrying capacity is limited. Many studies say that the world can accommodate between 8 and 16 billion people, which is both calming and distressing.

On the other hand, I believe that we shouldn’t be worried to such an extent about global population growth. This is because the Earth’s carrying capacity is still greater than the current global population. Also, a higher global population may also benefit the Earth’s economy and will provide a greater work force. In this way, a high global population may be viewed as an advantage to the world.

Overall, I believe that we should be worried about population growth. Considering the global population’s exponential growth, sooner or later, the Earth won’t be able to accommodate any more people. In the near future, I believe that certain measures must be taken to prevent the Earth from overpopulating, for example, a limit on how many children are born to a family. Back in 1980, the Chinese government implemented the one-child policy that limited Chinese families to only one child each, due to the country’s population growth being too rapid. China abolished this law in 2015, after realising that this law didn’t benefit the population in the way that it should have. This was because firstly, like most Asian nations, China has a traditional bias for sons, leading them to abort or murder baby girls to ensure a son. This led to a huge gender imbalance; in 2011, there were 34 million more men than women in China. Furthermore, due to many men not having a spouse, there was a great increase in crime rate. All this, of course, didn’t benefit the Chinese government at all. Hopefully, enforcing a global limit on children per family will be more effective than the Chinese one-child policy, and will slow down the global population increase and eventually steady it.
